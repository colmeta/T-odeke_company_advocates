General Questions

How much do lawyers charge in Uganda? +

Legal fees in Uganda vary significantly based on:

  • Case complexity: Simple matters cost less than complex litigation
  • Practice area: Corporate transactions differ from criminal defense
  • Attorney experience: Senior partners may charge more than associates
  • Time required: Some cases are billed hourly, others are flat-fee

We offer transparent pricing and free initial consultations to discuss costs upfront.

How long will my case take? +

Case timelines vary widely depending on:

  • Opposing party: Cooperation affects timeline

Practice Area Questions

How do I register a company in Uganda? +

Company registration involves:

  1. Name reservation with URSB
  2. Preparation of incorporation documents
  3. Filing with Uganda Registration Services Bureau
  4. Tax registration (TIN)
  5. Business licenses (if required)

We handle the entire process for you, typically completing registration in 2-4 weeks.

What should I do if I'm arrested? +

Immediately:

  1. Remain silent: Do not answer questions without your lawyer
  2. Request an attorney: You have this constitutional right
  3. Do not resist: Comply with officers physically
  4. Remember details: Note officer names, badge numbers, time
  5. Call us immediately: We provide 24/7 emergency representation

How is child custody determined in Uganda? +

Uganda courts determine custody based on the best interests of the child, considering:

  • Child's age and needs
  • Each parent's ability to provide care
  • Child's relationship with each parent
  • Stability of each home environment
  • Child's preferences (if age-appropriate)
  • Any history of abuse or neglect

Our family law attorneys fight to protect your parental rights while prioritizing your children's wellbeing.

What is the process for buying land in Uganda? +

Safe land purchase requires:

  1. Due diligence: Verify seller's ownership
  2. Title search: Check for encumbrances or disputes
  3. Survey: Confirm boundaries and size
  4. Purchase agreement: Legally binding contract
  5. Title transfer: Registration at land office
  6. Stamp duty payment: Government fees

We protect you from fraud and ensure clean title transfer.
